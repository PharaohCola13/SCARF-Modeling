Home View on GitHub Project Log Profiles

Found NASA reports relating to the electrical properties of lunar rocks and soil, as well as research defining the relationship between temperature and ground conductivity. There was also articles detailing the atmospheric properties of the moon that shed light on how to develop the profile. It appears that even through the moon's atmosphere is incredibility thin, it is primarily composed of Sodium, Potassium, Helium, and Argon gas. It was also mentioned that the Solar Weather interactions could alter the electrical properties of the surface since this weather is composed of ionized particles that produce magnetic fields. I am looking to now verify that a ground conductivity of 0.0001 S/m and a dielectric constant of 3 are appropriate parameters for the lunar surface.

    • March 14, 2019

    Was able to locate NASA research on the application of ITM models on the lunar surface.

    Lunar Approximations
    1. Ground Conductivity was approximated to be 0.0001 S/m
    2. The Dielectric constant was approximated to be 3.

    The comparison between the Longley-Rice, ITM, and ITWOM yielded results that favored the ITWOM model at distances <20 km. It was noted that the ITM model did not do so well in terms of line of sight measurements compared to the alternate models.
